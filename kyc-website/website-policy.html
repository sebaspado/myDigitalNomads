Website Privacy Policy

Introduction

This privacy policy explains how KYC.ME (the "Company", "We", or "Us") collects and uses information from users of its website and services at https://www.iqgp.io (the "Website", or "Site"). Users agree to the terms of the policy by browsing the website or using the services. The company processes personal data in accordance with legal requirements, only collecting what is necessary and retaining it for as long as needed. For questions about personal data processing, users can contact the company at legal@kyc.me.

Please refer to the KYC.ME Privacy Policy for details on the information collected, used, and processed for Identity Services, including identity verification, checks, and user authentication.

The policy may be updated periodically, and if there are any significant changes, you will be informed through notices on the website or via email to the address on record.

The Information We Collect

We collect personal information when you create a client account, purchase our services, provide services to KYC.ME, contact our team, visit our offices, engage with us on social media, or otherwise get in touch with us.

The information we collect on the website includes:

  • Personal Data - personally identifiable information includes your name, address, email, and telephone number, as well as demographic details such as age, gender, and interests. You provide this information voluntarily when registering on the website or participating in activities like online chat and message boards.
  • Derivative Data - when accessing the site, our servers automatically gather information including IP address, browser type, operating system, access time, and the pages you viewed directly before and after visiting the site.
  • Financial Data - when making a purchase or interacting with our services on the website, we may collect financial information, including your payment method details such as credit card number, card brand, and expiration date.
  • Mobile Service Data - device information, such as your mobile device ID, model, and manufacturer, and information about the location of your device, if you access the site from a mobile device.
  • Third-Party Data - information from third parties, such as personal information or network friends, if you connect your account to the third-party and grant the site permission to access this information.

Purpose of Collection, Use and Disclosure of Information

The accurate information we collect from you enables us to provide a smooth, efficient, and personalized experience. This includes creating and managing your account, processing transactions, delivering targeted advertising and newsletters, improving site functionality, monitoring usage, and contacting you for feedback. We also use this information to fulfill purchases, resolve disputes, and respond to customer service requests.

We may share information we have collected about you in certain situations. Your information may be disclosed as follows:

  • By Law or to Protect Rights - if we believe the release of information about you is necessary to respond to legal process, to investigate or remedy potential violations of our policies, or to protect the rights, property, and safety of others, we may share your information as permitted or required by any applicable law, rule, or regulation.
  • Business Transfers - we may share or transfer your information in connection with, or during negotiations of, any merger, sale of company assets, financing, or acquisition of all or a portion of our business to another company.
  • Third-Party Service Providers - we may share your information with third-parties that perform services for us or on our behalf, including payment processing, data analysis, email delivery, hosting services, customer services, and marketing assistance.
  • Affiliates - we may share your information with our affiliates, in which case we will require those affiliates to honor this Privacy Policy. Affiliates include our parent company and any subsidiaries, joint venture partners, or other companies that we control or that are under common control with us.
  • Marketing Communications - with your consent, or with an opportunity for you to withdraw consent, we may share your information with third parties for marketing purposes, as permitted by law.

Security of Your Information

KYC.ME takes safeguards designed to help protect information about users from loss, theft, misuse, and unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. While we have taken reasonable steps to secure the personal information you provide to us, please be aware that despite our efforts, no security measures are perfect or impenetrable, and no method of data transmission can be guaranteed against any interception or other type of misuse.

Retention

The company only retains information for as long as necessary to fulfill the stated purposes. Log data is kept briefly, while communications with individuals are retained for a longer period to continue providing ongoing service.

Your Privacy Rights

You have the right to exercise any of the rights described in this section by emailing legal@kyc.me. However, we may request verification of your identity and request before taking further action, as required by privacy laws. According to privacy laws, you have the right to:

  • Obtain Confirmation - as to whether or not your personal data is being processed.
  • Rectify Personal Data - to correct wrong information or supplement incomplete information.
  • Erase Personal Data or "Right to be Forgotten" - the right to erasure of personal data is not absolute and only applies if the data is no longer necessary for its original purpose, if there is an objection to the processing without overriding legitimate grounds, or if the data has been unlawfully processed.
  • Restrict Personal Data Processing - if you dispute the accuracy of your personal data and object to its erasure, but the data is needed to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims, you can request to halt processing while it is verified whether the legitimate grounds of the data controller override yours.
  • Be Informed - as to the rectification or erasure of personal data or restriction of its processing.
  • Data Portability - to receive your personal data in an appropriate format to be able to provide it to another party or transfer your personal data from one controller to another.
  • Not to be Subject to a Decision Based Solely on Automated Processing - unless necessary for entering into or performing a contract, authorized by law with measures to safeguard rights, or based on explicit consent, decisions about your data will not be made.
  • Lodge a Complaint with the Supervisory Authority - if you're a resident of the EU, please follow this link. If you're a resident of the UK, please follow this link.
